Acute sinusitis symptoms

Common signs include:

  • Pain, swelling or tenderness around your cheeks, eyes or forehead
  • A blocked or runny nose
  • A reduced sense of smell
  • Green or yellow mucus from your nose
  • A high temperature or feeling generally unwell

Is this service right for you?

Who this NHS service is for

  • Anyone aged 12 and over
  • Symptoms of acute sinusitis
  • No GP appointment or referral needed, just book or call in

When to get different help

  • Children under 12 should see a GP
  • Symptoms lasting more than a few weeks may need a GP review
  • A severe headache, swelling around the eyes or confusion need urgent help, call 999 or NHS 111

Questions people usually ask

Do I need to see my GP first?
No. You can come straight to the pharmacy. For sinusitis, a pharmacist can assess you and, where appropriate, supply treatment without a GP appointment.
Is it free?
The Pharmacy First consultation is a free NHS service. If you are supplied a medicine, the usual NHS prescription charge applies unless you are exempt.
Will I definitely be given medicine?
Not necessarily. The pharmacist assesses your symptoms and only supplies treatment if it is clinically appropriate. If it is not, they will explain the best next step.
Do I need an appointment?
You can book online for a set time, or call in. Booking ahead means you are seen quickly and in private.
